Exploring the Vertical Limits of Minecraft Bedrock Edition

Ever wondered how high you can go in Minecraft Bedrock Edition (MCPE)? One player, Jurydog8, put this to the test using creative mode and command blocks, revealing fascinating details about the game’s vertical limits.

Teleporting to the Skies

Using the command:

/tp @s ~ 100000000 ~

Jurydog8 expected to reach Y = 100,000,000 — but instead landed around Y = 30,000,000. Why? Minecraft silently limits coordinates to avoid crashing, even if the command looks valid.

Weird Sky Effects

At extreme heights, the game’s sky rendering breaks. Jurydog8 reported a pink sky above and blue below — a result of shader glitches when you're far outside the normal world height range.

Grappling Hook Glitch

Jurydog8 had built a command-based grappling hook system starting at Y = -60. But from 30 million blocks up, the command failed to work — likely due to Minecraft’s inability to calculate actions over such vast distances.

Why Y = 30,000,000 is the Limit

Minecraft Bedrock clamps both horizontal and vertical coordinates to about ±30 million blocks. This limit prevents crashes and bugs caused by extreme floating-point values and rendering issues.

What This Tells Us

  • Maximum build height is Y = 320
  • You can fly beyond Y = 320, but commands cap around Y = 30,000,000
  • Above this, commands and physics break down
  • Mobile devices like the HONOR X6 can surprisingly handle these heights

Next time you're in Creative mode, try pushing the limits — just don't forget to bring a way back down!
